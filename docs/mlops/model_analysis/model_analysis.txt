Note that in training a batch size of 256, and choosing k=20 out of them and
the batch of N items having only 1 positive (==relevant item) and the rest
being negative, we can estimate NDCG@20 for randomly choosing any of the N:
  random:
    prob of the single positive landing at any rank: (1/N)

    E[NDCG@20] = (1/N) * sum_{i=1,k}( 1/log2(i+1) )
               = 0.0275
  best:
    always has the single relevant at position 1:
    
    E[NDCG@20] = 1.0

For inference, we have top_k items and all are relevant and have different ranks.  
We want to calculate what NDCG@top_k would be for random ordering of the list.

   random: 
       each item has a probabaility of (1/top_k) of landing at any rank i.

       mu_20 = the avg relevance score of the top_k=20 items = (1/top_k) * sum_{k=1,top_k}(rel_k}
           = (1/top_k) * arithmetics series for adding 1 to 1 through top_k
           = (1/top_k) * (a_0*n + (n*(n-1)/2)*d) where a_0=1, n=top_k, d=1
           = (1/20.) * 210.
           = 10.5

       the total discount factor = (sum_{i=1,top_k}( 1/log2(i+1) )
           = 7.04

       for a random shuffle, every position is expected to have the mean relevant mu_20.
       E[NDCG@20] = mu_20 * tot_discount
                  = 10.5 * 7.04
                  = 73.92
