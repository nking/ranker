running on kaggle and trying to increase the use of GPUs
to decrease the overall runtime.

kaggle doesn't have Direct P2P nore NVLink/PCIe so GPU 
communication between one another on the T4x2 uses shared 
memory SHM.

For my iniital test of times on laptop, then Kaggle, I
used the elapsed time for a batch, and scaled those time
for estimates.  The problem with that approach is that 
the time estimates don't include dataloading backlog.
A better approach is to bootstrap from start to finish of
an HPO tune phas of 1 trial, but using num_epochs >= 4 to
get a more realistic use of memory.

Here are details of the first tune trial on Kaggle, showing
that the grain configuration I used may be using too many 
threads, leading to thread thrashing.  The GPUs are mostly
waiting for data.

kaggle times:
   batch _size 256, 473,000 train, 70,000 val
   10 epochs, 1 trial
   took 9.6 hours

   100 batches took 5 sec => 0.05 sec/batch
   0.05 sec/batch * (463000/256) batches  = 90 sec per epoch

epoch 0 : 1 = 36+16 = 52 min
      1 : 2 = 29+14 = 43 min
      2 : 3 = 25+31 = 56 min
      3 : 4 = 22+35 = 57 min
      4 : 5 = 22+38 = 60 min
      5 : 6 = 21+38 = 59 min
      6 : 7 = 27+39 = 66 min
      7 : 8 = 25+33 = 58 min
      8 : 9 = 22+35 = 57 min

NEXT attempt with a single train phase will use:
    grain_worker_count=2 \
    grain_read_options_num_threads=4 \
    grain_read_buffer_size=10 \
where before I had used 6, 8, and 50, respectively.
The long epoch runtimes might be from thread thrashing.

Note that the "elapsed time" in the logs is best to use for estimating times
for other runs rather than the batch to batch times.

Before running again on Kaggle,
  - have added prefetching put onto GPU of grain dataloader super padded graphs.
  - have added CPU logging.
  - have switched to faster gpu stats for logging.

Update after running on Kaggle with those changes:
   - the prefetching onto GPU helped tremendously, a factor of about 24
     10 epochs of training plus validation at each epoch took 25 minutes for the 463,000 sample dataset.
     1 epoch = 2.5 min
